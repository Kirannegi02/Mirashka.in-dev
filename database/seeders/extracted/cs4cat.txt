Case Study 1: Ensuring Compliance Confidence for a FinTech Startup
Service: Expert Legal & HR ConsultationsIndustry: FinTech (Startup)Company Size: 35 Employees
Background:A fast-growing FinTech startup faced compliance confusion with employment contracts, leave policies, and termination procedures as they rapidly expanded their team across multiple states. With founders focused on product development and investor relations, HR compliance was overlooked — leaving the company vulnerable to legal risks.
Challenges Identified:
Ambiguous employment contracts with missing statutory clauses.
No proper leave and attendance policy.
Misinterpretation of termination clauses under Shops & Establishment Act.
Founders lacked access to real-time legal guidance, delaying critical decisions.
Mirashka’s Solution:
Provided on-demand legal consultations, clarifying compliance requirements for each state.
Redrafted all employment contracts, aligning with labour laws and industry best practices.
Created a founder-friendly Compliance Guide, providing step-by-step HR legal checklists.
Set up a monthly legal review calendar to anticipate compliance risks proactively.
Results Delivered:✅ Reduced legal risk exposure by 70%.✅ Saved founders 10+ hours per month by eliminating legal guesswork.✅ Streamlined onboarding and exits, improving legal consistency across all hires.
Case Study 2: Structuring Policies for a Fast-Scaling E-commerce SME
Service: Policy & Documentation ExcellenceIndustry: E-commerce (MSME)Company Size: 120 Employees
Background:An e-commerce business with pan-India operations lacked formal HR policies, leading to inconsistent handling of leave requests, working hours, remote work norms, and disciplinary actions. This led to frequent employee grievances and rising attrition due to perceived unfair treatment.
Challenges Identified:
No comprehensive employee handbook.
Managers interpreted policies differently across locations.
Employees lacked clarity on benefits, holidays, and disciplinary measures.
New joiners received no structured policy orientation.
Mirashka’s Solution:
Developed a customized Employee Handbook, aligned with labour laws and the company's work culture.
Standardized policies across all locations — from leave and work hours to probation, rewards, and separation.
Introduced digital policy acknowledgment, ensuring every employee formally accepted policies during onboarding.
Conducted policy orientation workshops for managers to ensure uniform application.
Results Delivered:✅ Employee grievances reduced by 40%.✅ Faster dispute resolution as policies were documented and accessible.✅ New joiners reported stronger clarity during induction, improving onboarding satisfaction by 35%.
Case Study 3: Proactive Risk Management for a Manufacturing MSME
Service: Compliance & Risk AssessmentsIndustry: Manufacturing (MSME)Company Size: 85 Employees
Background:A mid-sized industrial manufacturing firm had grown organically over the years without formal HR audits or compliance reviews. When the factory underwent a surprise labour inspection, several documentation gaps were exposed, risking financial penalties and reputational damage.
Challenges Identified:
Incomplete employee files (missing KYC, PF, and ESI forms).
No formal Health & Safety policy in place.
POSH Committee not formally constituted despite 10+ female employees.
Outdated standing orders and employment terms.
Mirashka’s Solution:
Conducted a comprehensive HR compliance audit.
Created a detailed Compliance Heatmap, highlighting high-risk areas.
Standardized employee documentation across all departments.
Formulated workplace safety policies tailored to factory operations.
Established a compliant POSH Committee and conducted mandatory training.
Results Delivered:✅ Cleared follow-up labour inspection without penalties.✅ Achieved 100% compliance in employee files within 60 days.✅ Created a living compliance dashboard for leadership visibility.✅ Fostered proactive compliance culture with HR & Admin teams.
Case Study 4: Cultivating a Respectful Workplace for an IT Startup
Service: Workplace Ethics & Prevention Training (POSH Training)Industry: IT Services (Startup)Company Size: 45 Employees
Background:As a young, fast-growing IT startup, the leadership wanted to create a modern, inclusive, and safe workplace, especially with a growing number of women employees joining the team. However, employees were unclear about acceptable conduct, reporting mechanisms, and the company’s stance on workplace harassment.
Challenges Identified:
No formal POSH policy in place.
Employees unaware of their rights and responsibilities under POSH.
Managers lacked training on handling complaints sensitively and legally.
No Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) formed.
Mirashka’s Solution:
Drafted and implemented a POSH Policy, aligned with legal mandates and the company’s culture.
Conducted mandatory POSH Awareness Training for all employees (including case study discussions and roleplays).
Formed the company’s first Internal Complaints Committee (ICC), providing orientation training for committee members.
Created confidential reporting mechanisms with escalation matrices.
Integrated POSH acknowledgment into onboarding, ensuring all new joiners understood the policy upfront.
Results Delivered:✅ 100% of employees completed POSH training within 30 days.✅ Internal complaints committee trained and functional.✅ Improved employee confidence in workplace safety, reflected in engagement surveys.✅ Founders reported higher peace of mind knowing their workplace is legally compliant and culturally inclusive.
